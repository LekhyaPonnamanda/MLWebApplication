Dropout Preventive Measures

Early Intervention Programs

Implement early warning systems to identify students who may be at risk of dropping out based on factors such as age at enrollment, tuition fee payment status, and debt. Provide counseling and support to these students to address their challenges.

Financial Assistance

Offer financial aid and scholarships to students facing financial difficulties, especially those struggling to pay tuition fees. This can help reduce the burden of educational costs.

Mentorship Programs

Establish mentorship programs where experienced students or faculty members can provide guidance and support to new students. Mentorship can help students navigate academic challenges and stay motivated.

Academic Support Services

Provide access to academic support services such as tutoring, study groups, and academic advisors. These services can assist students in improving their academic performance and staying on track.

Engagement in Campus Activities

Promote student engagement in campus activities, clubs, and extracurricular events. Active participation in campus life can enhance the overall student experience and reduce feelings of isolation.

Personalized Academic Plans

Develop personalized academic plans for students who are at risk of dropping out. These plans should address their unique academic needs and provide a clear path to graduation.
